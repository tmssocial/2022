Dedicated to Taiwan

【Other Contributions】

　　Lifetime Dr. Mackay, who loves Taiwan deeply, has made great contributions to Taiwan in his lifetime. The first and most direct is the spread of the gospel, as a missionary, Mackay can be said to be quite successful.

▲ Mackay's missionary team／Source：The Research center for Digital Humanities


　　Secondly, Mackay did for the people to cure diseases and extract teeth and other deeds were also cliché. But Mackay’s dedication is not limited to this. For example, Dr. Mackay introduced foreign plant varieties, vegetables and fruits such as cabbage, cauliflower, carrots, beet roots, tomatoes, etc. And other tree plant seeds and sent to the public for free. Additionally, it has to be mentioned that the contribution to education, together with religion and medical care, is listed as the three great deeds of Mackay.

　　In addition to preaching, Mackay often gave lectures. First teaching his own disciples, and later establishing schools and advocating teaching concepts. Gradually influencing education not only in teaching. Mackay has set up the Oxford College and the Tamsui Girls' School in Taiwan. (For more details on both, please refer to the other sections of this website)

▲ the Tamsui Girls' SchooI／Source：He, Yao-Cheng


 　　At that time, Mackay introduced a Western-style education. Unlike the Chinese Four Books and Five Classics, he taught more modern knowledge of theology, science, society, nature and so on. In addition, Dr.Mackay also emphasizes great importance to women's right to be educated. At that time, Mackay found that under the immersion of thousands of years of Han Chinese culture in Taiwan. The concept of "women who lack talents is vituous” was deeply rooted in public. And "male superiority over female inferiority" made women at that time have no right to educated, let alone received God's grace.

　　Mackay began his work of equal rights for women, and he married Ms. Chang Tsung Ming, a Taiwanese woman, to help promote and oppose bad habits such as female foot binding and child brides. (Ms. Chang Tsung Ming was also a child bridesmaid) In the future, Mackay’s efforts enabled many women in Taiwan to get out of plight. And the Tamsui Girls' School founded by Mackay also cultivated excellent women.
