SU,WEN KUI

Interview name Interview with Teacher SU,WEN KUI
Interview date February 17 (Thu)
Interview time 10:00 a.m. to 12:00 p.m
Interview location Tamkang High School History Room
Interviewer SU, WEN KUI
Interviewee CHIU, JIA LE、HO, YAO CHENG
Recorder HSU, LANG, EN、CHENG , JEN-AN
Purpose of the interview Want to know more detailed historical records about Mackay, as well as related documents.

　

Foreword

　　First, introduce the interview characters: Su Wen Kui, the teacher who graduated from the art class of Tamkang Middle School, returned to Tamkang Middle School in 2000 to teach the history of the school, established the History Room of Tamkang Middle School, and served as the curator, and also served as a member of the Tamsui Literature and History Studio.

Mackay in the eyes of Teacher Su Wen Kui

　　In the eyes of the teacher, Mackay is a medical scientist, educator, but also an explorer, Mackay from the landing in Taiwan to before his death, was visiting Taiwan, a lifetime to the South more than 50 times, to Yilan more than 30 times, almost all on foot, a walk is 3 to 5 days, but also recorded the living conditions of many Han people and indigenous people, for the current historical research archaeology set the foundation.

The teacher believes that it can represent the building of Mackay

　　The first is the Tamsui Church because it is the first church founded by Mackay, which will be of great significance to Mackay, the second is that the Hobe MacKay Hospital is the first base in Taiwan it is the place where he first gives medical advice to others and it is also the birthplace of medical treatment, the third is the Oxford College, the introduction of Western education is the birthplace of the new doctrine, and the last is the Mackay Cemetery, which dedicated his life to Taiwan, and after his death, he was buried on the treasure island of Taiwan to represent his life.

What do you think the public may have some misunderstanding of Mackay?

　　Everyone regards Mackay as a god-like being, but Teacher Su Wen Kui feels that Mackay is also an ordinary person like us, and he is also a weakness of human nature, and when Mackay went to Houlong to meet a priest, the priest was frightened, because Mackay's language, accent, and skin color were completely a native of Taiwanese, and he had fully integrated into the Han society and culture.

Impressed by Mackay's contribution

　　Mackay is a missionary of the Presbyterian Church of Christ sent from Toronto, and the establishment of schools and hospitals in Taiwan is still used today, such as the Taiwan Theological College and Seminary founded by Mackay, the Tamsui Girl s ' School has become Tamkang Middle School today, the Oxford College has become the Aletheia University, and the Hobe Mackay Hospital has become the Mackay Memorial Hospital, which is of great help to today's society.

The establish a source of funding for Hobe Mackay Hospital

　　There were two sources of fundraising, the first is that there was a Presbyterian Church in Canada behind the support of the fundraising activities, and the second was a captain named Mackay in Detroit, USA, whose wife donated 2500 US dollars to Mackay, and the "Kai" of the Hobe Mackay Medical Center is named after the captain.

Mackay introduced quinine water

　　In cooperation with a British pharmaceutical company, quinine water and other drugs were sold to Mackay at a low price, so that Mackay was proud to save the Taiwanese, and after Mackay's story, the Presbyterian Church of Christ issued a certificate of thanks to the pharmaceutical company to thank them.

What is the reason today's churches disappeared?

　　Most of them were rented, the lease will be demolished as soon as it arrives, when Mackay was built, he did not expect that the church would be used for so long, when he was building it, he only used clay bricks and a few beams and columns to build up, and with time, the church was almost down, and there was the Sino-French War at that time, several churches were destroyed, but then Liu Ming Chuan gave the compensation of 10,000 yuan to Mackay, and Mackay used this money to build 7 churches.

Mackay and aborigines

　　Mackay's missionary spread to the Houlong Xingang Society, and once Mackay lived one night in the Pingpu tribe at Christmas, and later the natives of Xindian passed through Tamsui to see Mackay, and Mackay entertained them to live near the Oxford College for three days.
