From Toronto to Taiwan

▲ Home of Mackay(Canada)／Source：《Open Museum》


【Motivation for coming to Taiwan】

　　When Mackay was about 10 years old, he heard others describe the deeds of overseas missionaries, which aroused great interest in going to foreign countries, coupled with a sentence in Gospel of Mark, " Go into all the world and preach the gospel to all creation ", made him aspire to become a missionary from an early age, and at the age of 22, he went to the University of Toronto and Knox College for further study and transferred to Princeton Theological Seminary in the United States the next year for further study. After graduating and returning to Canada in 1970, he applied to the Overseas Missionary Society to become an overseas missionary and returned to his ancestral hometown of the University of Edinburgh Theological Seminary in Scotland to continue his studies during the waiting period.

　　After a nine-month wait, Mackay was finally allowed to become an overseas missionary, was designated to go to China as a missionary, and on October 19, 1870, left his hometown, all the way through Japan, Hong Kong, Guangdong, and Xiamen, and arrived in Taiwan at the end of December of that year to Takao (now Kaohsiung).

　　It was not his original plan to come to Taiwan, it was originally planned to go to Shantou, China, as his diary said: "It seems that there is an invisible line, leading me to this beautiful island." For Mackay, this is kind of God's will, so he is here to preach the gospel with all his heart.
