Crossing oceans to Taiwan

▲ Mackay and the visiting missionaries from Canada／Source：《Open Museum》


【Departure: Mackay crossed the ocean】

　　On October 19, 1871, Dr. Mackay officially embarked on a long journey, wondering if he knew that he would be enthusiastically dedicated to this mission, or whether he was ready to become prepared.

　　Mackay left his hometown by train and arrived in San Francisco on October 27, and a few days later, he took a ship to Yokohama, Japan, on November 27, and then to Hong Kong. In Hong Kong, Mackay and two members of the Church of the Presbyterian Church of England went to Guangdong, and under the advice of local members, they could go to Shantou to preach, but later found that there were already members in charge of preaching in the area, and finally, Mackay decided to go to Taiwan to start his missionary career.
 
　　Although the ship was not in good condition, Mackay arrived at Takao (first staged, not by today's Tamsui River) on December 30, 1871, and set off on New Year's Day of the following year, and finally took the boat at 3 p.m. on March 9, with his fellow Pastor Hugh Ritchie and Dr. Matthew Dickso officially arrived in Huwei (present-day Tamsui) When Dr. Mackay arrived, he described in his diary that he was touched by this place, admired Jehovah, and made up his mind to preach right here.
 

▲ Mackay Preaching to the Aborigines／Source：《Open Museum》


【Difficulty: Difficulty in Taiwan】

　　The house he originally rented was an abandoned horse barn, which he took good care of and became a place to live, hospital, and teach. He knows that to integrate into the local areas must learn the local languages. He made friends with cowboys on the outskirts of Tamsui and learned Taiwanese. He soon visited the local areas, using his feet to walk through the mountains and rivers of Taiwan, eating and sleeping, crossing tons of the mountains, and going deep into the Chinese and the original resident communities.
 
　　 At first, Mackay wanted to learn the Taiwanese, he first learned from his servants, imitating his voice, but his servants could not stand it and flee; so Mackay turned to the cowboy to learn, and learned to understand Chinese religion. One day a young man came to him to argue, and they debated several times, and the young man finally became a Christian, and later became the first priest set up by Mackay (that young man was Yan Qing Hua).

▲ Mackay, Yen Ching-Hua and the women of the Pingpu tribe／Source：《Open Museum》


　　Taiwan is an island-type country that is often threatened by typhoons and earthquakes, and most people worship many gods to ensure safety. In a society where there is a mixture of polytheistic beliefs in seven points of Taoism, two points of Buddhism, and one point of Confucianism, Mackay wants to promote the belief in the "one God", and the degree of difficulty can be imagined! In 1872, the port authorities issued a circular prohibiting locals from renting houses for foreign missionary purposes. The local forces of the people were also quite unfriendly to the mission of Mackay, and at that time, the local leaders of the three surnames of Monga once threatened: "If there are three surnames, there is no Jesus religion; if there is Jesus religion, there are no three surnames.", expressing a posture and determination that is incompatible with Jesus’s teachings.

　　Rev. Hugh Ritchie not only learned the Taiwanese language from this missionary predecessor but also knew from Rev. Hugh Ritchie e that although the population in Northern Taiwan was large, no missionaries went there. Therefore, driven by the enthusiasm of the mission, Mackay decided on his next destination.

 

　　 Although Pastor Mackay was knowledgeable in medical treatment, he was not a licensed doctor, and formal medical treatment invited foreign doctors hired by the Tamsui Customs and Taxation Department to visit the Hobe MacKay Hospital and invited local Chinese doctors as assistants. He invited five foreign doctors: Dr. S.Ringe, Dr. C.H.Johansen, Dr. B.S.Rinnie, Dr. F.C.Angear, and Dr. W.Wikson, who presided over the " Hobe MacKay Hospital " and made indelible contributions to modern medicine in Taiwan, especially Dr. Mackay and Dr. S.Ringe dissecting a sailor who was fatal to the respiratory tract in the medical hall in 1879 when they dissected a sailor who was fatal to the respiratory tract in the medical center, which was the first case in the world.
