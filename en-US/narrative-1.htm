Project overview

Category：
Local Leaders
A brief introduction of the category：
　　Pastor Kai Rui Li, who’s name translates originally into George Leslie Mackay, arrived in Taiwan 150 years ago, and since then began his missionary journey in Tamsui, where he landed. Pastor Mackay's influence is not limited to the Tamsui area, and even has extensive and far-reaching influence in the northern part of Taiwan. Pastor Kai Rui Li was repeatedly rejected and humiliated during the early days of his mission due to the fact that foreigners were not understood and accepted among the locals, but the situation improved with the arrival of the first disciples. In the following 30 years, Dr. Ma Kai devoted himself to mission work and was instrumental in the development of Christianity in northern Taiwan. His descendants and the students he cultivated also made significant contributions and had tremendous local weight. Dr. Mackay is a significant figure in Taiwan's religious history, for being the first western missionary to bring Christianity to northern Taiwan.
A brief description of the topic：
　　The title of this thematic research project is "The Legendary Life of Mackay in Taiwan". We would like to review Dr. Mackay's contributions in the fields of religion, education, and medical care in the northern Taiwan region (especially the Tamsui district) through literature research and interviews. In addition, we also hope to commemorate the 150th anniversary of Mackay's mission to Taiwan through the creation of a website, as well as promote the great spirit of Dr. Mackay's love for Taiwan and allow more people to acknowledge his resilience and selfless love.
Internet Environment：
● School： The school is divided into elementary, junior high, and senior high schools, with a total of 5 computer classrooms, each with about 50 computers for the entire school. The entire school network is connected to the TANET, with a speed of 300Mbps for upload/download in both directions.

● Personal： Most of the team members have laptops and desktops at home, and use ADSL to access the Internet. When we cannot meet in person, we discuss through communication software and use cloud drives to transfer data to each other.
Obstacles we had to overcome：
　　The biggest roadblock we encountered in the beginning was the technical aspect of the project, due to our inexperience in operating art design software, and we didn't understand the programming needed to set up a website either, but we overcame the technical problems thanks to the guidance of our seniors. In addition, we also encountered the bottleneck of insufficient literature material during the process, which we solved by interviewing experts on the subject of our research. Thanks to these experts and teachers, who altruistically provided their views and opinions, we were able to acquire information that wasn’t available on the internet and address the lack of data. However, the biggest problem we encountered during the whole project was "lack of action", and because of this, the progress of this research project was close to being stagnant for a long time. We really appreciate the schoolteachers and seniors who didn’t give up on us despite our slacking and helping us complete all the website content, one step at a time.
Project Evaluation：
　　It was a very honorable and unforgettable experience for us to represent the school at the International Schools Web Expo 2022. We encountered great obstacles in locating materials, interviewing experts, and conducting on-the-spot investigations at first as a result of the covid-19 pandemic over the last year. When confronted with these issues, we worked together to tackle them one by one. This process allowed us to understand that teamwork often stimulates the incredible potential of the group, and the results achieved by collective cooperation often exceed the sum of the individual achievements of the team members. In addition, the theme of our website is "The Legendary Life of Mackay in Taiwan". The reason for our selection of this topic for research was because Mackay's eldest son, Kai Rui Lian (George William Mackay) actually founded Tamkang High School, the school in which we study in, and Dr. Mackay's cemetery is also located on Tamkang’s campus. For all Tamkang High School students, Dr. Mackay is more than just a character in history textbooks, he and his family are actually part of our campus life. In addition, coincides with the 150th anniversary of Mackay's missionary mission in Taiwan, so by participating in the International School Internet Expo competition, we hope to educate more people about the extraordinary life of Dr. Mackay.
